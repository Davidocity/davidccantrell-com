## About Me

My name is David Cantrell. I first came to Japan in 1988, just after college. At the time, Japan had one of the most dynamic economies in the world—and a culture that drew me in. I moved to Tokyo to work for the Honda Motor Company and to experience Japan firsthand. What began as a job ended up shaping the course of my life.

Japan never left me.

I hold a bachelor's degree from Harvard University and an MBA from UCLA. Over the next 30+ years, I built a career in digital marketing in the United States while returning to Japan regularly—for work, for travel—and watching the country evolve. My professional work required understanding how people make decisions and what information they trust.

In January 2025, my wife and I moved to Kumamoto, her hometown on the island of Kyushu. This time, it isn't a visit.

I live here now.

## About KyushuDave

KyushuDave is where I document my experiences living and traveling in Kyushu, Japan.

I write every article. I take every photograph (unless noted otherwise). I visit every place I recommend.

**What you'll find here:**
- Detailed guides to Kyushu's seven prefectures
- Hiking trail reports from mountains I've climbed
- Food and drink recommendations based on meals I've eaten
- Honest assessments—including what didn't work

**What you won't find:**
- Content written by freelancers or AI
- Recommendations for places I haven't been
- Sponsored content disguised as editorial (all partnerships are disclosed)

## Why Japan?

I became interested in Japan in college in the mid-1980s. I studied its history, literature, sociology, and business alongside my main coursework. That academic interest turned into lived experience when I moved here in my early twenties.

Since then, Japan has been a constant thread through my life—professionally, personally, and now as a place to live.

There's a difference between visiting a place once and returning to it over 30 years.

I've seen the Japanese economy rise, fall, and stabilize. I've watched its culture influence the world. And I've seen the country become one of the most popular travel destinations on earth.

What keeps me here is simpler: I'm still curious.

## Why Kyushu?

This is where I live.

I've lived in Tokyo. I've spent time in Kyoto. Both are remarkable—and widely covered.

Kyushu is different.

It's less visible to the outside world. The pace is slower. The landscape is more physical—active volcanoes in Aso and Sakurajima, dense forests in Yakushima, coastal hot springs in Beppu, and small cities that still feel rooted in place.

I've hiked Mt. Kuju, Mt. Karakuni, and Mt. Nakadake. I've soaked in onsen across Oita. I've eaten my way through Fukuoka's yatai and Kumamoto's basashi bars.

I experience it not as a destination, but as somewhere to live.

This site is, in part, an attempt to document that.

## Why I Started KyushuDave

After a long career building and scaling digital businesses, I wanted to create something of my own—something slower, more deliberate, and grounded in direct experience.

KyushuDave is where I do that.

It's a place to explore Kyushu through writing and photography, and to build a body of work over time.
